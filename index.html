The Problem

The data gap blocking green finance

Banks want to fund solar. Developers want to build. The numbers never line up — because the data to connect them doesn't exist in a bankable format.

01 / GAP

No standardised bankability data

Lenders cannot independently audit solar yield — creating full dependency on expensive consultants and 6-week due diligence timelines.

02 / GAP

SMEs locked out of capital

Smaller solar developers can't access institutional lending because prohibitive due diligence costs make their projects commercially unviable.

03 / GAP

No automated scoring tool exists

No programmatic infrastructure exists for financial institutions to score solar credit risk in real-time — anywhere in Sub-Saharan Africa.

Besa Masaiti

Founder & CEO — Rehoboth Research LLC

Besa holds an MSc in Financial Engineering from Lehigh University and brings hands-on expertise across AI/ML architecture, quantitative research, blockchain analytics, and renewable energy finance. She is the architect of the Rehoboth Solar Engine — combining XGBoost credit scoring, geospatial data pipelines, and a Swarms-based Policy AI Agent to deliver real-time bankable intelligence.

As the first recipient of a Botswana National Sports Council elite scholarship to study in the United States, Besa brings a lived understanding of both the opportunities and constraints shaping Southern African development. She has engaged with the Botswana Economic Transformation Programme (BETP) Secretariat on a technical feasibility partnership and spoke at ETHDenver 2026 on tokenized securities and the U.S. housing crisis.

Besa is also a FIDE Woman Chess Master and founder of an annual international women's chess tournament in Botswana — representing the country at two Chess Olympiads.
